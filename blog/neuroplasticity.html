One thing to understand about neuroplasticity in this context is that it works through a process called synaptic pruning - as we gain new experiences, come synaptic connections become stringer while others are eliminated. Neurons that are used often develop stronger connections. Those that are rarely or never used eventually die. This pruning process allows our brains to adapt to changing environments. Letting this happen helps promote the ability to learn new things, enhance existing cognitive capabilities, recover from strokes or traumatic brain injury and strengthening areas where function is lost or has declined.

Simply having the knowledge that scientifically, our brains can change and adapt gives us confidence and motivation to dive into learning new things. We can also adopt learning strategies specific to how our brains work in order to develop skills and retain information. In addition to learning strategies. the principles of neuroplasticity promote lifestyle changes for improved brain health, which can lead to a better state of mental well-being.

One way I have been engaging with the principles of neuroplasticity so far is when i have been using data from real world things I already know about, such as the rules of a complex card game so that an existing pathway is being strengthened with new information on how to write code.

The main things I will integrate into my learning plan to benefit from the principles of neuroplasticity are getting enough sleep, spending some time socialising, regular exercise to increase blood flow to the brain and maintaining a healthy diet.

One thing I will focus on is viewing my mistakes as opportunities to learn.
